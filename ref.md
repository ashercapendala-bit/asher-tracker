## Push Day — Mon & Thu (~40 min)

*5 min warmup + 30 min work + 5 min core*

### Incline Push-Up
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 12–15
- **Rest:** 60s
- **Cue:** Hands on bench or step. Full chest down, elbows at 45°. Builds the movement pattern before going flat.
- **Progression:** Drop incline angle every 1–2 weeks. Remove bench entirely when 3×15 feels easy.

### Flat Push-Up
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × AMRAP minus 2 (stop 2 short of failure)
- **Rest:** 90s
- **Cue:** Lock out at top, chest touches floor at bottom. You can do 15 — aim for quality, not grinding.
- **Progression:** Add 1 rep per set per week. When you hit 20+ reps, diamond push-ups progress.
- **Push B from week 5:** 4 sets

### Diamond Push-Up
- **Sets × Reps:** 2 × 6–8 (3 × 8–10 once flat push-up hits 20+)
- **Rest:** 90s
- **Cue:** Hands form a diamond directly under chest. Heaviest tricep load in the session. Strict form over reps.
- **Progression:** Build to 3×12 by week 8.

### Pike Push-Up
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 8–10
- **Rest:** 90s
- **Cue:** Hips high in an inverted V, forehead toward floor. Your shoulder strength foundation — no equipment overhead pressing equivalent.
- **Progression:** Elevate feet on bench from week 5. Push B from week 5: 4 sets.

### Tricep Dips (Chair)
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 10–12
- **Rest:** 60s
- **Cue:** Hands behind on chair, legs extended. Elbows track back — never flared. Straight spine throughout.
- **Progression:** Add 2-second pause at bottom from week 4.

## Pull Day — Tue & Fri (~42 min)

*5 min warmup + 32 min work + 5 min core*

### Dead Hang
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 20–30s (build to 45s by week 6)
- **Rest:** 60s
- **Cue:** Depress shoulder blades slightly — don't just dangle passively. Active hang. Builds grip endurance and lat awareness.
- **Progression:** Wk 1–3: 20–30s | Wk 4–5: 30–40s | Wk 6–8: 40–45s

### Scapular Pull-Up
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 8–10
- **Rest:** 60s
- **Cue:** From dead hang, depress and retract shoulder blades without bending elbows. Small movement — you rise only a few cm. Fixes the sticking point at the bottom of the pull-up.
- **Progression:** Add 2-second hold at top of each rep from week 3.

### Negative Pull-Up (Overhand)
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 4–5 (4 sets from week 5 on Pull B)
- **Rest:** 2 min
- **Cue:** Step or jump to get chin over bar. Lower as slowly as possible — target 6 to 8 seconds down. This is the single most effective pull-up builder. Control the whole way.
- **Progression:** Wk 1–4: 5–8s | Wk 5–8: 8–10s. Add 4th set (Fri) from week 5.

### Chin-Up (Underhand)
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × max quality reps (4 sets from week 5 on Pull B)
- **Rest:** 2 min
- **Cue:** You have 4 — aim for 3 clean reps per set. Don't grind the last rep with bad form. Full range: dead hang at bottom, chin clearly over bar at top. No kipping.
- **Progression:** Add 1 rep to at least 1 set per week. Add 4th set (Fri) from week 5.

### Inverted Row (Under table / low bar)
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 10–12
- **Rest:** 90s
- **Cue:** Body rigid at 45°, pull chest to table edge. Squeeze shoulder blades at top. The horizontal pull that directly transfers to pull-up strength.
- **Progression:** Lower body toward horizontal as strength builds. Add 3-second pause at top from week 6.

### Towel Pull-Apart
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 15
- **Rest:** 45s
- **Cue:** Hold a rolled towel at eye level, pull apart to full extension. Rear delt and external rotation health — protects the shoulder joint under all this pulling volume.
- **Progression:** Slow to 3 seconds each direction when it gets easy.

## Core Block — Every Session (8 min)

Add this after every push or pull session.

### Dead Bug
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 8/side
- **Rest:** 45s
- **Cue:** Opposite arm and leg lower slowly to just above floor. Lower back stays glued down the entire time. Breathe out on the way down.
- **Progression:** Add 3-second pause at full extension from week 3. Build to 5-second pause.

### Hollow Body Hold
- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 20–30s (build over 8 weeks)
- **Rest:** 30s
- **Cue:** Arms overhead, lower back pressed into floor, legs raised. If lower back lifts, bend knees slightly. The gymnastics core base.
- **Progression:** Wk 1–2: 20–25s | Wk 3–4: 25–35s | Wk 5–8: 35–45s

### Plank / Plank Shoulder Taps (from week 4)
- **Sets × Reps:** 2 × 30–45s (or 10 taps/side from week 4)
- **Rest:** 30s
- **Cue:** Elbows under shoulders. Squeeze glutes and quads. Don't let hips sag or pike.
- **Progression:** Move to shoulder taps from week 4 — tap each shoulder alternately, keep hips still.

## Running — Wed & Sat

### Run Types

**Easy Run (E)**
Conversational pace — you can speak full sentences throughout. If you can't, slow down. This is your fat-burning, aerobic base session. Basketball background means you'll want to push — resist it completely on easy days.

**Run/Walk (RW)**
3 min run / 1 min walk, repeated for the full session duration. Not a failure — this is how aerobic base is safely built. Protects shins in weeks 1–2 while legs adjust to road loading.

**Short Intervals (SI)**
90s hard (7–8/10 effort) / 90s walk recovery, repeated 6–8 times. Structured like basketball conditioning — your body knows this format. Warm up with 3 min easy walk first.

### 8-Week Running Plan

| Week | Wednesday | Saturday |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | 25 min Run/Walk (3 min run / 1 min walk) | 25 min Run/Walk |
| 3–4 | 28 min Easy Run (continuous) | 25 min Easy Run |
| 5–6 | 30 min Easy Run | 6 × 90s hard / 90s walk |
| 7–8 | 35 min Easy Run | 8 × 90s hard / 90s walk |

### Running Watch Points

**Shin splints** — Court sport loads the shin differently to road running. In weeks 1–2, if shins are tender after a run, drop back to run/walk and rest 48 hours. Never push through shin pain — it becomes a 3-week setback.

**Going too fast** — Easy runs should feel almost embarrassingly slow at first. That is correct. If you cannot hold a conversation, you are running too hard.

**Leg fatigue** — Pull days don't load the legs. Push days don't either. If legs feel heavy on a run day in weeks 1–2, that's normal and resolves as fitness builds.

## Nutrition

### Daily Macro Targets

| | Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Training days (Mon–Sat) | 1800 kcal | 145g | 145g | 60g |
| Rest day (Sunday) | 1650 kcal | 145g | 110g | 55g |

**Priority order:**
1. Hit 145g protein every single day — protects your 31.1kg of muscle in a deficit
2. Stay within calorie target
3. Carbs and fat are flexible within the totals — if a meal runs higher fat, trim carbs

**Carb timing:** Eat the bulk of your carbs in the meal before and after training. Evening meal is lower carb.

**If weight stalls 2 weeks in a row:** Drop calories to 1700 by reducing carbs by 35g. Keep protein at 145g. Don't go below 1600 kcal.

**Stays in:** Fruit (banana, strawberries), plain Greek yoghurt, oats, rice, potato, stevia and monk fruit sweetener.

## Progress Tracking

**Weigh every Tuesday morning:** after waking, after bathroom, before eating or drinking. Same conditions every week.

**7-day average:** Add your last 7 daily weigh-ins and divide by 7. This smooths out water retention from salty food or hard training sessions. The average is the real trend — not the single Tuesday number.

**Expected loss:** 0.6–0.9kg/week at 1800 kcal with this training load.

**If stalled:** No movement in 7-day average for 2 consecutive weeks → reduce calories to 1700 (cut carbs by 35g, keep protein at 145g).

**Week 8:** Book an Evolt rescan. At 82kg with 31.1kg skeletal muscle, the scales will underreport fat loss if any muscle has been added. The scan shows the real body composition shift.
